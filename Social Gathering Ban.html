Social Gathering Ban

1. Introduction

Reducing social contact is key to delaying the spread of COVID-19 in Hong Kong. The Government will publish in the Gazette today (March 28) the enactment of the Prevention and Control of Disease (Prohibition on Group Gathering) Regulation ("Regulation") (Cap. 599G) to take decisive and severe measures Coronavirus disease 2019 outbreak.

The Regulation will come into effect midnight on March 29 for a period of three months. According to the Regulation, the Secretary for Food and Health will publish a notice in the Gazette today. The ban on group gatherings of more than four people in public places will come into effect at midnight on March 29 for a period of 14 days.

The Regulation empowers the Secretary for Food and Health to prohibit group gatherings of more than 4 people in public places during specified periods for the purpose of preventing, preventing, delaying or otherwise controlling the cases or spread of specified diseases..

2. Reastaurant

This Regulation will come into effect on March 28, 2020.The regulations are as follows

  • The number of customers in a restaurant shall not at any one time exceed 50% of the number of seats.
  • The arrangement of tables in any catering premises must ensure that there is at least a distance of 1.5 meters between any 2 tables used or being used by customers in the premises or there is some form of partition to effectively separate them.
  • No more than 2 persons shall be seated at one table in any catering premises.
  • Any person who is on any catering premises must wear a mask at all times except when eating or drinking on the premises.
  • Taking the body temperature of a person before allowing him/her to enter the catering premises.
  • At any catering premises, hand sanitiser must be provided to persons on the premises.
  • No dine-in from 5 to 6 in the evening.

3. School

Face-to-face classes are suspended, schools can teach through online courses.

5. Exempted groups

  1. A group gathering for or related to transportation
  2. Group gatherings for the performance of government functions
  3. Group gatherings to fulfill the duties of a statutory body or government advisory body
  4. Group gatherings for working at the workplace
  5. Group gatherings for providing, obtaining or receiving hospital or healthcare services in a healthcare facility
  6. Group gatherings of people living in the same household
  7. Group gatherings necessary for legal proceedings in a court, magistracy or tribunal
  8. Group gathering necessary for proceedings in legislative council or district council
  9. Group gatherings at funerals, or where there is mourning or tribute to a deceased person who has not yet been buried or cremated, including near the place where the deceased died or where a fatal injury occurred Nearby, sacrifices or ceremonies held to mourn the death of the ancestors
  10. Group gatherings of no more than 20 people at a wedding, provided no food or drink is served at the wedding
  11. A group gathering at a meeting of a body if the meeting is required to be held within a specified period in order to comply with any Ordinance or other regulatory instrument governing the operations or affairs of the body
  12. Group gatherings for the dissemination of information or techniques useful in the prevention and control of specified diseases (or for the handling of supplies or articles useful in the prevention and control of specified diseases)

7. Punishment

Anyone who takes part in a prohibited group gathering, organizes a prohibited group gathering, or owns, controls or operates a place where such a gathering takes place and knowingly allows such a gathering commits an offense and is liable to a maximum fine at level 4 ($25,000 ) and imprisonment for six months. A person who participates in a prohibited group gathering can discharge liability for the offense by paying a fixed penalty of $2,000.

Later, the government announced that the fixed penalty for violating the gathering restriction order will be increased from $2,000 to $5,000 to achieve the required deterrent effect. In response to the increase in the fixed penalty, the maximum penalty upon conviction has also been increased accordingly. The government stated that it will strengthen inspections and take severe enforcement actions against those who ignore the regulations. If it is found that the situation of compliance with the regulations is not satisfactory, it cannot be ruled out that further penalties will be imposed.

The "Prevention and Control of Disease (Prohibition on Group Gathering) Regulations (Cap. 599G)" related to the gathering restriction order was launched as early as March. Various government departments have made a total of 450,000 inspections, nearly 60,000 warnings, and more than 4,700 Fixed penalty tickets, and 47 prosecutions. The ticket control rate of the "restriction order" is about 1%, that is, a ticket will be issued for every 100 inspections.

Media surveys and expert observations have found that many places do not strictly abide by the government's epidemic prevention restrictions, including restaurants and bars that are full of occupancy, violating the occupancy limit, and many diners take off their masks during non-eating hours and talk loudly, making it very easy for the virus to spread. diffusion. As for the hotel, it has even become a place for some unconscious citizens to escape the restrictions on gatherings and hold parties. They use the so-called staycation as an excuse to gather people in hotel rooms to carnival. It can be seen that even if the government departments have made regulatory complaints, the places that can be monitored are limited. In private places, the self-discipline of citizens is mainly relied on. Therefore, the restriction order cannot cover group gatherings in all places.
